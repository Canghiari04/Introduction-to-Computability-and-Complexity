Formalizing what we have seen so far, we can say.
\begin{definition}
    Let $H$ be a finite \textbf{hypothesis class} or \textbf{space} and we consider a consistent hypothesis $h \in H$. If $h$ has high true error, namely the 
    probability it matches all $m$ samples is small, and considering an union bound over $H$, where large hypothesis classes increase the risk of 
    \textbf{overfitting}, we get:
    \begin{itemize}
        \renewcommand{\labelitemi}{-}
        \item Smaller $H \rightarrow$ guarantees better generalization.
        \item Sample complexity depends on $log|H|$.
    \end{itemize}
\end{definition}
