\begin{example}
    e.g. Suppose our instance class is $X = \bigcup_n X_n$ where $X_n = \{0, 1\}^n$. \vspace{7pt}

    $1^{st}$ Example. \\
    One first example of a representation class for $X_n$ is the class $CL_n$ of all conjuctions of literals on the variables $x_1, \dots, x_n$. \vspace{7pt}

    As an example, the conjuction 
    $$x_1 \wedge \neg x_2 \wedge x_4$$
    defines a subset of $\{0,1\}^4$. \vspace{7pt}

    $2^{nd}$ Example. \\
    A second example of a representation class for $X_n$ is the class $\text{CNF}_n$ of CNFs over $x_1, \dots, x_n$, which are \textbf{conjuction} 
    of \textbf{disjunctions} of literals.
\end{example}
