First of all, we introduce the notion of \textbf{function}:
\begin{center}
    what a function really is?
\end{center}

\begin{definition}
    Given two sets X and Y, such that:
    \begin{center}
        $X \cdot Y=\{(x,y) | x \in X,y\in Y\}$
    \end{center}
    a \textbf{relation} is a subset of the cartesian product. \vspace{3pt} \\
    A function from X to Y is a relation described as:
    \begin{center}
        $f \subseteq X \cdot Y$.
    \end{center}
    It's a subset of the cartesian product, such that $\forall x \in X$ there is one $y \in Y$ such that:
    \begin{center}
        $(x,y) \in f$ 
    \end{center}
    and we state as follows:
    $f: x \rightarrow y$.
\end{definition}
