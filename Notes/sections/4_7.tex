The answer to the question suggested by the title is \textbf{no}: all the problems in NP are not equivalent. For instance, the following two problems are not
equivalent, even though they are both in NP:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Maximum independent set, a problem that we already know to be NP.
    \item The language of palindromes is easy to be proved in P, so it's also in NP.
\end{enumerate}
But, anyway, they are different: the degree of difficulty in proving these problems in NP is different. \textbf{Therefore, how can we distinguish problems in NP 
that have different degrees of difficulty?} \vspace{7pt}

For sure, we cannot do so much knowing that a decision problem $L$ is in NP, we need something else. However, we can look to the complexity class NP as a tool 
to study the relative difficulty between distinct problems. The relative difficulty between problems is expressed by a \textbf{pre-order relation} between 
languages.
